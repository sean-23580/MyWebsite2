Top 12 Tourist Spots in the Philippines

1. Boracay Island (Aklan)

It is a world-famous tropical paradise known for its white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife.

Fun Facts:

White Beach is 4 km long and super fine – like powder!

It was once closed for 6 months in 2018 for environmental rehabilitation.

2. Chocolate Hills (Bohol)

It has over 1,200 symmetrical mounds that turn brown in the dry season, resembling chocolate kisses.

Fun Facts:

The exact origin is still debated – some say they’re coral deposits!

Legend says they’re tears of a heartbroken giant.

3. Banaue Rice Terraces (Ifugao)

Ancient terraces carved by hand over 2,000 years ago by the Ifugao people.

Fun Facts:

Often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World."

They're still used today to grow rice and vegetables.

4. Mayon Volcano (Albay)

A nearly perfect cone-shaped volcano famous for its beauty and frequent eruptions.

Fun Facts:

It's the most active volcano in the Philippines.

Local legend tells of a tragic love story behind its shape.

5. Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park (Sulu Sea)

A protected marine sanctuary and a diver's dream with vibrant coral and diverse marine life.

Fun Facts:

It's only accessible by liveaboard boats during March to June.

Home to over 600 fish species and 360 coral species!

6. Puerto Princesa Underground River (Palawan)

A spectacular 8.2 km-long underground river flowing through caves.

Fun Facts:

You can explore it by paddle boat.

It’s one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature.

7. El Nido (Palawan)

Famous for dramatic limestone cliffs, turquoise lagoons, and pristine beaches.

Fun Facts:

Some scenes from "The Bourne Legacy" were filmed here.

There are over 40 islands to explore.

8. Vigan (Ilocos Sur)

Description: A UNESCO site with Spanish-era architecture and cobblestone streets.

Fun Facts:

You can ride a kalesa (horse-drawn carriage) around town.

It's known for Vigan longganisa (local sausage) and bagnet (crispy pork).

9. Siargao Island (Surigao del Norte)

Description: A laid-back island famous for surfing, especially at Cloud 9.

Fun Facts:

It's not just for surfers—there are lagoons, caves, and island hopping too.

It’s been named one of the best islands in Asia multiple times.

10. Baguio City (Benguet)

Description: The "Summer Capital of the Philippines" due to its cool mountain climate.

Fun Facts:

Known for strawberries, pine trees, and Ukay-Ukay (thrift shops).

The Panagbenga Festival showcases flower floats and street dancing.

11. Taal Volcano (Batangas)

Description: A volcano within a lake, with another lake inside its crater – like a volcano-ception!

Fun Facts:

It's the smallest active volcano in the world.

You can get amazing views from Tagaytay’s ridge.

12. Cebu (Cebu Province)

Description: A historic island with a mix of beaches, waterfalls, and city attractions.

Fun Facts:

Magellan first landed here in 1521.

You can swim with whale sharks in Oslob or visit Kawasan Falls.
